Driver Commentary
HR for Health — +$0.04M (+18%)
Strongest MoM contributor. Net +38 ERs (397 → 435) driven by 46 gross adds against minimal churn. At ~$600 ACV/ER, this partner generates outsized revenue per add and continues to significantly exceed its contractual minimum. YoY ARR is up 27× from $0.01M in Feb 2025 — reflecting the full ramp of a maturing, high-value partnership.
HiBob — +$0.03M (+50%): Contractual Minimum Step-Up
The 50% jump is mechanical: the minimum fee stepped from $5,000/mo to $7,500/mo per the signed ramp schedule. No material ER growth occurred (18 ERs, flat MoM). Key PSM question: is utilization on a path to exceed the minimum before the next contractual step?
Lettuce — +$0.02M (+13%): Strongest Gross Adds Month
87 gross adds in February (net +81 ERs, 518 → 599) — best intake month on record. Lettuce is fully usage-based off its minimum floor, so every ER is incremental revenue. Worth confirming with PSM whether this reflects a new steady-state intake rate or a one-time cohort.
Heard / Vagaro / Freshbooks — Steady Organic Growth
Each added 38–44 net ERs in February. ER add rates normalized from January's elevated switching-season intake, consistent with seasonal patterns. On a YoY basis, all three are up significantly: Heard +85%, Vagaro +67%, Freshbooks +87%. The February moderation relative to January is expected, not a signal of weakness.
GoCo — -$0.00M (-0.1%): Seasonal Normalization, Not a Concern
February deceleration (8 ER adds vs. 70 in January) is consistent with GoCo's historical Q-start pattern — January, April, and July reliably see intake spikes as clients switch payroll providers, and subsequent months normalize sharply. February 2025 showed the same pattern (4 adds after 38 in January). YoY, GoCo is up +154% in ARR ($0.30M → $0.75M). Watch April for the next expected intake spike.
Minimum-Floor Partners (Chase / Lattice / US Bank / BQE) — Flat at $0.86M Combined ARR
These four partners are locked at contractual minimums, contributing $0.86M in ARR. The ER base is building (combined +82 adds in February: Chase +54, US Bank +24, BQE +3, Lattice flat), but utilization has not yet reached the minimum threshold for any partner. Each represents meaningful upside when usage breaks through the floor.
Groundcloud — -$0.01M (-7.7%): Continued Attrition
No new adds for a second consecutive month; residual ER churn continues eroding the base (73 ERs in Feb 2025 → 58 in Feb 2026, -21% YoY). ARR has declined from $0.11M to $0.09M YoY. This appears to be a runoff dynamic absent a specific re-engagement plan.
